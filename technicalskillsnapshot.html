Technical Skills

Experience with collection management systems

  • ArchivesSpace is a website for organizing and making collections' finding aids accessible.
  • Archivists’ Toolkit is an open source archival data management system that promotes data standardization and efficiency.
  • Customer Relationship Manager (CRM) system helps track different paper donors.
  • Optical Character Recognition (OCR) is used to convert images of printed text into machine-readable text. Adobe Acrobat and ABBYY FineReader are software that can be used to do OCR.
  • Handwritten Text Recognition (HTR) is the conversion of images of handwritten text to machine-readable text, and is more specialized than OCR. Transkribus is a software that can be used to do HTR.

Knowledge of library and archives professional standards, such as:

  • Describing Archives: A Content Standard (DACS) is an archival description standard first adopted by the Society of American Archvists (SAA) in 2004. The standard contains 11 principles of archival description.
  • Encoded Archival Description (EAD) is a standard for encoding finding aids using XML online, hosted by the Library of Congress.
  • Library of Congress Subject Headings (LCSH) were originally created for the use of Library of Congress, but have become a standard for describing records.
  • The Art & Architecture Thesaurus (AAT) is a list of terms maintained by the Getty used for cataloging, linking, retrieval, research, and accessibility.

Collections development skills (ALA)

  • Implement the principles and methodologies of collection development including establishing goals and priorities, researching and documenting provenance and collection history, conducting assessments and appraisal of collections, creating and adhering to collection development policies and goals based on knowledge of user needs, existing collections and mission, deaccessioning when appropriate, and appreciating the relationships between the collection material, its content, and its monetary and research values.
  • Employ effective and ethical acquisition methodologies according to institutional and professional standards and values.
  • Establishes and maintains effective relationships with vendors, organizations, communities, individuals, and colleagues for the purpose of developing collections; educates document creators and stewards about the importance of and proper practice of preserving material.
  • Identifies materials, in all formats, appropriate for a special collection, based on criteria documented in institutional collection development policies, and based on the institution’s mission, including format, rarity, scarcity, age, physical and intrinsic characteristics, condition and stability, market value, historical and cultural significance, teaching and research value, and assessed and/or expressed user needs; selects materials for digitization using the same criteria, when appropriate.

Predicted growing demand for particular skills (from interview)

  • In this position, you need to be both a collector and a processing archivist
  • People will need to be nimble in multiple skill sets as more repositories lose funding and close
  • Archivists should be able to work with the public to fill the role of both the curator and the archivist
